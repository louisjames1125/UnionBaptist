Our History

Union Baptist Church has faithfully served the communities of Clinton and Howard Counties for generations. Founded by a small group of dedicated believers, the church has grown from humble beginnings into a vibrant congregation committed to preaching the Gospel, nurturing faith, and serving the community.

Through times of growth, renewal, and challenges, Union Baptist Church has remained rooted in Christ-centered worship and biblical truth, standing as a living testament to God’s faithfulness across generations.

Moments Through the Years

  • 1851:  Middlefork of Wildcat Baptist Church established.
  • 1861:  Renamed Middlefork Regular Baptist Church.
  • 1871:  Dedication of new church house.
  • 1880:  Church bell purchased and belfry erected.
  • 1881:  Organ purchased for worship.
  • 1882:  Graveyard Committee assigned to care for cemetery.
  • 1885:  First recorded missionary meeting at the church.
  • 1906:  First election of church and Sunday School officers.
  • 1908:  First Homecoming service held.
  • 1936:  Church officially renamed Union Baptist Church.
  • 1937:  Basement added beneath the church building.
  • 1948:  Baptistery installed.
  • 1960:  Groundbreaking and completion of the parsonage.
  • 1965:  Church destroyed by a tornado.
  • 1966:  First services held in the rebuilt church building.
